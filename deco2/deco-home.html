The Art of Decoupage

What is Decoupage?

Decoupage comes from the French word “découper” meaning "cutting up” or “cut-out”. It came to be used for the technique of decorating objects with cut up pieces of paper glued to the surface, although it can be traced back to a variety of countries including Siberia, China, Italy, Germany, Poland, and of course France.

In traditional decoupage decorative paper is applied to an object which is then coated with varnish for a seamless inlaid effect. Since its origins, it’s been adapted into many different evolutions, including paper tole (3D decoupage), decopatch, and reverse decoupage. Its effects can also be achieved digitally, although a bit unconventional.
